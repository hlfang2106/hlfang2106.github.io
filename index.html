About me

I am a tenure-track Assistant Professor in the School of Mathematical Sciences at Peking University. Previously, I was a Van Vleck Visiting Assistant Professor at the University of Wisconsin-Madison, working with Prof. Xianghong Gong. I received my PhD in 2018 in the Department of Mathematics at Rutgers University–New Brunswick, working with Prof. Xiaojun Huang. I received my Bachelor degree (2012) in mathematics from Tsinghua University, working with Prof. Ben Andrews.

Research interest

I have been working on several complex variables, algebraic geometry, complex geometry, and differential geometry. My current interests are harmonic analysis on homogeneous varieties and mathematical physics.
