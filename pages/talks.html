  • Title: Concepts of Geometry in Condensed Matter Physics
    Xu Yang (The Ohio State University), 7/27, 7/29, 7/31, 8/2, 8/4, 8/6, 8/8, 20:00-22:00

    Course Overview: In this mini-course, I will be focusing on the role of geometry in condensed matter physics. I will develop necessary mathematical techniques in solving physics-motivated problems along the way. Topics including van Hove singularities, topological insulators, response theories and quasi-crystals are discussed. And mathematical tools of Morse theory and algebraic topology are introduced. This course is aiming at providing students with a perspective of the role of geometry in condensed matter physics. Students with a general physics and math background (~level of sophomore) and a certain degree of mathematical maturity are welcome.

    Suggested Reading:

    1. Morse Theory, J. Milnor
    2. Solid State Physics, N. Ashcroft and D. Mermin
    3. Many-Body Physics, Topology and Geometry, S. Sen and K. Gupta
    4. Geometric Phases in Physics, A. Shapere and F. Wilczek (ed.)
    5. Topology in Condensed Matter, M. I. Monastyrsky

    Course contents: I will be focusing on the following topics.

    1. Introduction to Morse theory: Morse inequality
    2. Physics of van Hove singularities: quantum oscillations, Lifshitz transitions, shape of Fermi surfaces
    3. Weyl semimetals and the idea of Berry phases
    4. Insulators: crystals, quasi-crystals and topological defects
    5. Insulators: band topologies

    Homework Policy: There will be homework problems to help students getting familiar with ideas and calculations. In addition, I would recommend students to write a term paper about interesting topics related to geometry in condensed matter physics. I will provide some references if you don’t have any idea what to explore.
